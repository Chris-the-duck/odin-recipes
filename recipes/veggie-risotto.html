Veggie Risotto

(This is the general idea, but without the peas. Ick.)

Description

Creamy risotto is always enjoyable. This one once again involves a lot of eyeballing amounts according to taste and will work with pretty much any green veggies you like.

I don't put peas in this because I don't like them, but feel free to if you must.

Ingredients

  • Risotto/Arborio rice
  • Butter
  • Vegetable stock
  • A bit of white wine
  • Onion (1)
  • Lemon
  • Garlic (as much as you like, I use 1-2 cloves)
  • Frozen broad beans (can use fresh but why bother peeling them?)
  • Whatever green vegetables you want. I like to use courgette and asparagus.
  • If there's any savoy left over from the minestrone, that goes well in here too
  • Otherwise you can also add spinach, but my husband's not a huge fan
  • Cheese (I use cheddar; parmesan isn't vegetarian but if that isn't an issue, go for it)

Steps

  1. Peel and chop the onion
  2. Put some olive oil and butter in a large pan on medium heat until the butter's melted
  3. Add the onion and cook until softened
  4. Add the rice and fry until it's all covered in the butter/oil and the smell is slightly nutty
  5. Add the wine, stir and cook until the liquid has evaporated
  6. While the wine is cooking off, peel and dice the garlic and add
  7. Season with pepper
  8. Add lemon zest if available, or a couple splashes of lemon juice
  9. Add a couple ladlefuls of stock and let simmer
  10. Meanwhile, wash/cut/prep all other veg you are using
  11. I like to saut√© the other vegetables in olive oil in a separate pan and get some browning on the courgette, but this step is optional
  12. Add frozen beans to rice
  13. Add other vegetables to rice
  14. Keep stirring and adding more stock a couple ladlefuls at a time until the rice is cooked and the risotto looks creamy
  15. Grate as much cheddar as you like into it and stir
  16. Check seasoning and enjoy!
